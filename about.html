About DSP Records

DSP Records (Dyrtie Sound Productions) started as an underground events and artist support project and evolved into a record label built for dark rooms, loud systems, and real connection.

Origins — Dyrtie Sound Productions

DSP began life in the early 2010s as Dyrtie Sound Productions, a small but relentless underground promotion brand. The mission was simple: bring bass-heavy music into proper venues, support local DJs and performers, and build nights where people walked in as strangers and left as part of the same story.

Those early years were about hauling gear, negotiating with venues, and throwing parties for people who cared more about sound systems and community than dress codes or bottle service.

Events & Milestones

Over time, DSP grew into a trusted name on flyers and lineups across Alberta and beyond:

  • Liquid Nightclub — Medicine Hat, AB
    New Year’s Eve 2013 “Black & White Masquerade” and multiple bass-driven nights featuring DJs, dancers, live performers and full-room production.
  • Whiskey Creek Nightclub — Medicine Hat, AB
    DSP-hosted takeovers focused on underground bass and drum & bass, with packed dancefloors and high-energy crowd interaction.
  • Defunk x DSP — Liquid Nightclub
    Collaborative shows supporting touring headliners like Defunk while showcasing local support acts and building the regional scene.
  • Sal’s on 17th — Calgary, AB
    Infamous Productions x DSP Halloween events bringing DJs, costumes and heavy sound to one of Calgary’s busiest strips.
  • Aurora Nightclub — Banff, AB
    DSP & Tech Noir Productions teaming up for nightlife events in the Rockies, connecting local crews and visiting artists.
  • UK Radio Takeover — COVID Era
    During the COVID lockdowns, DSP organized a Canadian takeover of a UK-based radio station. On the last Sunday of every month, DSP curated up to twelve DJs from across Canada to broadcast live into the UK airwaves, keeping the underground connected when dancefloors were closed.

These nights, broadcasts and memories are the backbone of the DSP story: real people, real sound systems, real connection.

From Events to Record Label

After years of focusing on live events, DSP began shifting toward recorded music. The same drive that filled rooms started moving into studios, production sessions and focused release planning.

DSP Records was born as the label side of that journey — a place to release system-driven underground music with the same ethics that built the events:

  • Artist-first — fair, transparent and built around long-term development.
  • Scene-driven — contributing to the culture, not extracting from it.
  • Underground always — quality over hype, momentum over cheap shortcuts.

Today DSP Records runs a structured release schedule across digital platforms, pairing studio work with artwork, visualizers and long-form mixes meant to be heard front to back — not just skipped through one track at a time.

Where DSP Is Going Next

The label is a chapter, not the ending.

  • Relaunch DSP-branded events and takeovers with a stronger catalog behind them.
  • Develop DSP Sound Labs / DSP Studios as a physical hub for DJs and producers.
  • Grow a roster of artists who share the same underground values and long-term vision.
  • Connect the full pipeline from demo submissions to releases to live shows and tours.

DSP Records exists for people who still believe in dark rooms, loud systems and real connection. The story started in the clubs and it will circle back there — with a full label, catalog and community behind it.
